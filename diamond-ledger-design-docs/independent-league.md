# Independent League — v0.1 (Future, Speculative)

*Not in the game at launch — framework for future expansion, same status
as the International Club Championship doc. Directly formalizes and
enriches the "unaffiliated clubs" concept already sketched in the Minor
League System doc (fragile finances, no subsidy, higher fold/relocate
risk, adoptable by expansion teams as instant affiliates) rather than
inventing something new — this doc is that concept given real shape.*

## Structure: Multiple Regional Confederations, Not One League

- **Loose, organically-forming regional leagues — plural, not a single
  national independent league.** Mirrors real independent baseball
  directly (the American Association, Atlantic League, Frontier League,
  Pioneer League all coexist as genuinely separate organizations covering
  different US regions, not one unified structure).
- Local/regional travel only — genuinely semi-pro in flavor and budget,
  a real step down from even the Rookie League's already-modest scale.
  "Yeoman teams," per Christopher's framing — scrappy, local, not
  polished operations.
- Can form, merge, dissolve, and reform over time — much more organic
  and fluid than the fixed affiliated pyramid. This instability is a
  feature of the tier, not a flaw to design away.

## Player Population: Where They Come From

- **Washouts from the affiliated system** — Rookie League players who
  hit the 24-age/3-service cap without promotion (`rookie-league.md`),
  minor league free agents (Player Pathway doc), released/non-tendered
  players, undrafted college free agents. A real landing spot that keeps
  the "long tail" of the generated player population alive and
  narratively relevant, rather than players simply disappearing into an
  abstract free-agent void once they wash out.
- **Occasional hidden gems** — players whose *true* potential (Player
  Attributes & Development doc) was under-read by the affiliated system's
  scouting (Scouts doc), who get a second chance to be discovered. A
  genuine narrative payoff for the existing scouted-vs-true-potential
  mechanic, mirroring real independent-ball-to-MLB success stories.
- Mostly **marginal talent that stays marginal** — consistent with the
  broader "most players simply have lower ceilings" population
  philosophy (Player Attributes & Development doc). The hidden gems are
  meant to be rare, not a hidden second tier of stars.

## Team & Player Churn

- **Teams fold, relocate, and reform far more often** than anywhere in
  the affiliated pyramid — genuinely precarious finances, no safety net
  (see Financial Model Tie-In below).
- **Players move between independent teams more freely** than the
  option/rights system governing affiliated ball — no equivalent
  structure holding them in place, matching how transient this level of
  the sport actually is in real life.

## Path Upward: Becoming an Affiliate

A thriving, popular independent team can become an actual minor league
affiliate "from time to time" — this directly extends the Minor League
System doc's existing Contraction, Expansion & Forced Re-Affiliation
section, where an expansion club (or a club that's lost an affiliate)
can adopt a nearby unaffiliated club as an instant affiliate.
**Independent League teams are literally the pool that mechanic already
draws from** — this doc formalizes and enriches that connection with real
texture (regional confederations, churn, hidden gems) rather than
introducing a new system alongside it.

## Financial Model Tie-In (not designed here)

Independent teams operate entirely outside the established Financial
Model — revenue streams, salary floor, luxury tax (Financial Model:
Revenue/Expenses docs) were all built around the affiliated pyramid and
don't apply here. Independent finances would need their own, much
simpler and much more fragile model: tiny market-size-driven revenue
only, no revenue sharing, no protections. Not designed now — flagged as
real future work, not attempted here.

## Simulation Depth (open question)

Given the emphasis on churn and instability, Independent League probably
warrants an even lighter simulation than the "basic sim" already defined
for AAA/AA/A/Rookie (Minor League System doc) — possibly closer to the
abstracted approach used for college, given how organizationally unstable
this tier is meant to be. Not decided — flagged as open for whenever this
actually gets built.
