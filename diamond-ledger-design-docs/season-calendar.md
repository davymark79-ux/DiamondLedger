# Season Calendar — v0.6

*Reconciles every calendar-dependent open item scattered across other
docs: trade deadline (Player Movement), roster expansion (Commissioner
Vision), the Cup's two-season span (In-Season Tournament), League
Playoffs vs. MLB2 Championship timing, and WBC placement (International
Tournament). This is a structural sequence, not literal calendar dates —
exact dates are a separate, later pass once this ordering is confirmed.
v0.5 confirms a reduced 150-game regular season (down from 162), which
substantially eases the doubleheader crunch — see Regular Season Length
below.*

## Full Sequence

*Worked example using anchor dates Christopher proposed — Spring Training
March 1, Opening Day April 1 (non-WBC year) — resolves to: regular season
ends ~October 3, League Playoffs conclude ~mid-November (Scenario A,
recommended — see Remaining Open Items for the full calculation and why
it beats the alternative). These are planning estimates, not a locked
calendar.*

**Preseason**
1. Offseason activity concludes (free agency, Rule 5 draft — see
   Offseason section below).
2. Pitchers & Catchers report — **shortened from real life**, per
   Christopher: modern year-round player fitness reduces the ramp-up
   Spring Training actually needs.
3. Full squads report; Spring Training games proceed, overall shortened
   from real MLB's typical length.
4. **WBC seasons only (every 4th season):** tournament held at the tail
   end of Spring Training / start of the regular season window.
   **Confirmed: WBC is always preseason, never mid-season** — an earlier
   draft of this doc incorrectly implied WBC could also land in July
   alongside other mid-season events; that was an error, corrected here.
   Opening Day shifts **about a week later** in WBC seasons league-wide
   (real-world precedent), giving participants time to reintegrate with
   their clubs. **Confirmed: WBC can delay the start of the season, but
   never shortens it** — the full 150-game count (updated from an
   earlier 162-game draft, see Regular Season Length below) is protected
   regardless;
   a delayed start means the season runs later into the year and/or
   leans more on doubleheaders, not fewer games.

**1st Half**
5. **Opening Day.**
6. Regular season proceeds, interspersed with the **previous season's Cup
   knockout weekends** — Play-In, Round of 16, Quarterfinal, Semifinal
   (not the Final) — each on a Fri/Sat/Sun block, spread throughout the
   1st half. No regular-season games during these blackout weekends
   (existing Tournament Roster Rule).
7. **Domestic draft — early June**, positioned after the notional
   "college season" concludes (college itself is abstracted/unsimulated
   per the Player Pathway doc, but the calendar placement still respects
   that logic).
8. Minor league season is **shorter than the majors'** — confirmed to
   mirror real-world MiLB structure: **AAA ~150 games, AA ~138 games, A
   ~132 games** (adapted from the real 4-level split, since this project
   uses a single "A" level rather than real MiLB's High-A/Single-A
   split). Ends before the majors' regular season, feeding the Active
   Roster Expansion trigger later.

**All-Star Break (1 day longer than real life, per Christopher)**
9. **Cup Final** — single game, day before the All-Star Game, at the
   All-Star host city. This concludes the *previous* season's Cup cycle
   entirely.
10. **All-Star Game.**
11. **Hall of Fame induction** — confirmed placement, same weekend as the
    All-Star Game (matches real MLB's July tradition). This makes
    All-Star weekend a genuinely stacked showcase — Cup Final, HOF
    induction, and the All-Star Game all clustered together — confirmed
    as an intentional "biggest weekend of the year" design rather than a
    scheduling conflict to avoid.

**2nd Half**
12. 2nd half begins.
13. **New Cup group stage** — 3 Fri/Sat/Sun weekends, **front-loaded
    early in the 2nd half** rather than spread evenly or backloaded. This
    is a deliberate scheduling choice, not arbitrary: playoff-bound clubs
    get a clean stretch run without a competing tournament obligation
    right before the games that matter most for positioning.
14. **Trade deadline** — placed **after the first of those three
    group-stage weekends.** Confirmed position, not a placeholder.
15. Remaining 2 Cup group weekends complete early in the 2nd half; rest of
    the 2nd half is normal regular-season play building toward the
    stretch run.
16. **Active roster expansion** triggers once the minor league season
    ends (not a fixed calendar date, per Christopher).
17. Regular season ends — **150 games, confirmed** (reduced from an
    earlier 162-game draft — see Regular Season Length below).

**Postseason**
18. **MLB2 Championship** — Foundry2 vs. Exchange2, single game, held
    **1-3 days after the regular season ends**, explicitly **not
    synchronized to MLB1's Playoff timeline** — it's a single game, no
    reason to wait.
19. **MLB1 League Playoffs** begins — Wild Card (best-of-5) → Divisional
    (best-of-7, reseeded) → Championship Series → Pennant Series → Finals
    (Foundry pennant winner vs. Exchange pennant winner). This is a much
    longer process than MLB2's, running independently alongside/after it.
20. **MLB1 Finals conclude** — this is the true end of the season.
    **Regular-season awards** (MVP, Best Pitcher, Gold Glove, Silver
    Slugger, ROY, MOY) are announced shortly after the regular season
    ends, ahead of/around the start of the postseason. A **separate
    Finals/Championship MVP award** — not yet formally added to the
    Awards & Hall of Fame doc, flagged as a needed addition — is
    announced immediately after the Finals conclude, matching the
    real-world tradition of naming a Series MVP on the field right after
    the last out.

**Offseason** — confirmed to otherwise mirror real life:
- Free agency opens; non-tender deadline falls early in the window.
- **International draft, confirmed as an actual draft (not a signing
  period), with a two-step structure:** Draft Day → a short signing
  window → **Signing Day** deadline. A team that drafts a player has
  until Signing Day to actually sign him; **unsigned draftees become free
  agents.** Timing confirmed: **mid-January, after the Winter Meetings
  (December) but before Spring Training.** This tightens the "drafted via
  the international draft" branch of the Player Pathway doc's 3-year
  international pathway rule — that branch now specifically means drafted
  *and signed by Signing Day*, not just drafted. Distinct from the
  domestic draft's draft-and-follow model (where rights persist through a
  multi-year college career) — international draftees are presumably
  further along and ready to sign now, so a short window makes more
  sense than years-long held rights.
- **Hall of Fame voting** — results announced in January, matching real
  MLB. (Induction itself moved to All-Star weekend, see above.)
- Rule 5 protection deadline, then the **Rule 5 Draft at the Winter
  Meetings (December)** — confirmed placement.
- **Winter Meetings also bring a real spike in trade activity** — not a
  new mechanic, just narrative emphasis: expect a concentrated burst of
  trade-rumor and trade-completed League Wire stories during this window,
  giving the Writers Corps a natural high-activity stretch.
- Arbitration figure exchanges — January, matching real MLB.
- **CBA term structure, confirmed:** term length itself is negotiated
  between the parties (can run longer or shorter as they decide), but
  **expiration always lands in the offseason**, pinned relative to the
  Finals rather than a fixed calendar date — e.g. a 5-year deal expiring
  "5 days after the Finals conclude." Keeps CBA transitions from ever
  landing mid-season regardless of how long a given agreement runs.

## Worked Estimate: Off-Days & Doubleheaders (Non-WBC Year)

Answering Christopher's direct question, and simultaneously the first
real pass at the "game-count validation" item flagged below.

- Real MLB baseline for a team: **~24 total off days** across a ~186-day
  season (~162 games) — mostly scattered single travel days plus the
  ~4-day All-Star break itself.
- This calendar adds, on top of that baseline: **12 days** (4 Cup
  knockout weekends × 3-day blocks, 1st half) + **9 days** (3 Cup
  group-stage weekends × 3-day blocks, 2nd half) + **1 day** (the extra
  ASB day) = **22 additional blackout days.**
- **If the season keeps roughly real MLB's total span (~186 days),** a
  team ends up with **~46 total off days** across the season, and needs
  **~21-22 doubleheader days** just to still fit all 162 games — since
  each doubleheader recovers exactly one lost game-day without adding a
  calendar day.
- This is a genuinely large number relative to real baseball (which sees
  maybe 0-2 doubleheaders a year per team, almost always rainout
  makeups) — worth reacting to deliberately rather than discovering as a
  surprise later, since it makes fatigue management (Injuries doc) a
  near-constant concern rather than occasional.
- **The lever that trades against this directly: letting the season run
  longer than real MLB's ~186-day span** reduces the doubleheader count
  roughly one-for-one with each additional calendar day allowed. Not yet
  decided which way to lean — flagged as a real, deliberate choice.
- This is order-of-magnitude reasoning based on the sequence above, not a
  precise day-by-day schedule (which doesn't exist yet) — treat as a
  planning estimate, not a final count.

## Regular Season Length: Reduced to 150 Games (confirmed)

Confirmed reduction from 162 to 150 games — real MLB's 162 is treated as
vestigial to this project's own calendar needs, not a number worth
preserving for its own sake. Recomputing the worked estimate above with
150 games instead of 162, using a **realistic** rest allowance (not the
artificial zero-rest exercise) — a real-MLB-equivalent ratio of ~24 rest
days per 162 games, scaled to 150 games ≈ 22 rest days:

| | 162 games | 150 games |
|---|---|---|
| Zero-rest surplus (Apr 1 – Nov 15 latest) | 8 days | 20 days |
| Realistic rest days needed | 24 | 22 |
| Doubleheaders still needed with realistic rest | **16** | **2** |

**150 games essentially solves the scheduling crunch outright** — down
from 16 doubleheaders even under a realistic rest allowance, to just 2.
This is a genuinely different foundation than the 162-game version this
doc originally worked from, and makes the whole calendar meaningfully
healthier, not just marginally better.

Minor league seasons scaled proportionally (150/162 ratio applied to the
real-world lengths already established in the Minor League System doc):
**AAA 150 → 139, AA 138 → 128, A 132 → 122.** See that doc for the
update.

## Remaining Open Items

Most of the original gaps are resolved above. What's genuinely still open:

1. **Season length vs. doubleheader count — resolved with real dates.**
   Worked with fixed anchor dates (Spring Training March 1, Opening Day
   April 1) to compute both scenarios concretely:
   - **Scenario A** (season stays close to real MLB's ~186-day span,
     ~22 extra doubleheaders absorb the Cup blackout days): regular
     season ends **~October 3**, playoffs conclude **~mid-November**
     (using real-world pacing to estimate the 5-round League Playoffs
     bracket at roughly 6 weeks).
   - **Scenario B** (no extra doubleheaders, season extends instead):
     regular season ends **~October 25**, playoffs conclude **~December
     6**.
   - **Recommended: Scenario A.** December 6 is a real practicality
     problem, not just an aesthetic one — deep winter for most of the 25
     markets, many with open-air stadiums (Stadiums doc), colliding
     directly with cold-weather gameplay effects (Weather doc) in a way
     that goes well past "chilly October game" into genuinely
     implausible outdoor baseball. Mid-November is still later than real
     MLB's early-November finish, but stays physically plausible.
     **Confirmed direction: lean into the higher doubleheader count.**
   - Caveat unchanged from the original estimate: this is
     order-of-magnitude reasoning (24-day real MLB baseline, ~6-week
     playoff estimate from real-world pacing), not a precise day-by-day
     schedule — treat the specific dates as planning estimates to refine
     once an actual calendar gets built, not final.
2. **CBA negotiation windows** — years when a new agreement is being
   hashed out presumably need real estate somewhere in the offseason
   (real MLB: negotiations typically conclude by Opening Day, sometimes
   with lockout drama beforehand) — the *expiration timing* is now
   resolved (pinned to days-after-Finals, see Offseason above), but the
   negotiation process itself isn't addressed yet.
3. **Finals/Championship MVP award** — flagged above, needs to be
   formally added to the Awards & Hall of Fame doc, not just named here.
4. **Future, speculative: International Club Championship** — see the
   `international-club-championship.md` doc, now with a full framework
   (country ratings, slot allocation, group/knockout structure). Revised
   from an earlier "must live in the offseason" recommendation — since
   only a handful of clubs ever participate, it only needs to block out
   those specific teams rather than the whole league, so it can live
   in-season after all (timed to conclude before the postseason
   positioning stretch, same principle as the Cup's group-stage
   front-loading). Still explicitly a future concept — no other
   countries' leagues exist yet.
